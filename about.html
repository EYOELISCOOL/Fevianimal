About FeviAnimals

Who We Are & Why We Exist

FeviAnimals protects vulnerable animals and promotes animal welfare across Ethiopia through rescue, education, and community compassion.

Our Story

Compassion, one street and one shelter at a time.

FeviAnimals began with a simple observation: across Ethiopia's cities and rural communities, countless dogs live without shelter, medical care, or protection under the law. We exist to change that — not with pity, but with practical, sustained action.

We believe animal welfare and human wellbeing are deeply connected. A community that protects its animals is safer, healthier, and kinder for everyone in it — including its children.

From our first rescue in Addis Ababa to our advocacy work in Hossana, we've seen what happens when people choose compassion. We're building a movement — and we'd love for you to be part of it.

Urgent: Hossana Mass Killings

Hundreds of Dogs Killed in Hossana After Rabies Outbreak

In July 2026, following the deaths of three children from rabies, community groups in the central Ethiopian town of Hossana forced residents to kill their own dogs — including many that had been vaccinated. Here are the facts.

"People dragged dogs out and killed them in shocking ways. This is immoral and unacceptable in religion, culture, and law. Videos show owners crying as their dogs were killed."
Alaazar Kebede, local veterinarian
Source: BBC News (2 July 2026)
"We estimate that 400 to 450 dogs were killed in just a few days."
Alaazar Kebede, local veterinarian

What happened

  • Community groups ordered residents to kill their dogs — vaccinated or not — under threat of arrest and a 50,000 birr fine
  • Some owners hanged or beat their dogs to death; others handed them over to be killed
  • Mayor Samuel Shigute called the killings "illegal" and said they were not ordered by his administration
  • Police stopped the mass killings within a day, but reports say pressure on dog owners continues
  • In Ethiopia, it is illegal to kill animals in public spaces or cause them to suffer in cruel ways
